Objectives and Motivation

  • Marathi learning is a luxury and one has to decide if he/she can afford to teach it to children. Begin by reading "Objectives and Motivation" for parents and teachers. Let us know if you can add any more items.

    Also, read Good teaching qualities: inducing interest and positive expectations by Premchand Palety.

Devanagari alphabet learning
(recognition of letters, writing method, and sounds)
  • http://www.sudhanwa.com/marathi/marathi.htm Animated Devanagari writing including bArAkhaDI.
  • http://www.avashy.com/hindiscripttutor.htm. Animated Devanagari writing and audio.
  • http://members.tripod.com/sarasvati/devanagari/alphabets.html (allow pop-ups.) Animated Devanagari writing.
  • https://sanskritdocuments.org/marathi/alphabet.html list and audio.
  • http://acharya.iitm.ac.in/lang_scripts/animations/intro.php, well developed animaltion based Devanagari characters and information. Audio clips are included.
  • all Devanagari letters in one page with static direction for writing.
  • Script writing in Wikipedia.com.
  • http://www.bookofindia.com/ letters, conjunts, and audio for bArAkhaDI.
  • Find an interesting bArAkhaDI watch at http://www.inditoy.com/barakhadi/watch/ for help in writing all the letters with mAtrA/vowel-modifier along with audio for letters and sample word. The dial on the watch changes the mAtrA. The color coded pronunciation help is grouped by letters. (The site is developed at present for Marathi and Hindi learning with products available on on-line paid subscription for use or purchase. Yogini Dahiwadkar and Madhavi Sabnis, IndiToy Inc., Columbia, MD, US.)
  • Learning for kid project at http://www.sudhanwa.com/marathi/e-learning.htm initiated by Sudhanwa Joglekar and Umesh Jere. The site gives animated writing of Devanagari vowels, consonants with bArAkhaDI, and numbers. It has been linked from the all-encompassing school4all.org project site under Marathi akShar oLakh.
  • The Jawarlal Nehru University, Sanskrit department has a set of learning tools and scripts that allows audio of alphabets and derived Hindi words with pictures.
Marathi learning links:
  • Why learn Marathi?, a motivational article by Kamatkar family at marathimitra.com.
  • marathimitra.com/, perhaps main resource for Marathi learning on the web. The transliteration is little unusual for some letters tv for ta, dv for d.
  • Maay-Marathi learning resources, requires naidunia fonts.
  • Marathi rhymes with some audio.
  • Learn Marathi at punediary.com. English-Marathi words, Common phrases.
  • English-Marathi
  • Learn Marathi Quick Guide, a Maharashtra tourism site. Has articles, images, screen savers, video, and wallpapers among other information.
  • Marathi grammar, a reference by Professor Damle. Resource Center For Indian Language Technology Solution (CCFILT, IIT Mumbai). Requires to download DVTTYogesh font and select VIew/Encoding/User Defined in IE.
  • Also, at CFILT, see Marathi shAbdabandha, a searchable Marathi - English dictionary, more than a conventional Marathi dictionary. It gives different relations between synsets or synonym sets which represent unique concepts.
  • The Aryabhusan school Marathi-English online dictionary, originally (1911) written by Shridhar Ganesh Vaze.
  • Marathi English Primer, excellent preparatory book by Ganesh Hari Bhide, 1901 edition at archive.org.
  • mAhArAShTra bhASheche vyAkaraNa by Dadoba Pandurang, 1850 edition.
